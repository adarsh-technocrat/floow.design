## What Happened

On March 19, 2026, Google unveiled a major redesign of its Stitch AI design tool via Google Labs. Originally launched at Google I/O 2025 as a simple text-to-UI generator, Stitch has been rebuilt from the ground up as what Google calls an "AI-native" design platform. The update introduces a concept the company is calling *vibe design* — letting users describe business objectives, emotional intent, or design inspirations in plain language rather than drawing wireframes or specifying individual components.

## What's New in Stitch

The March 2026 update ships five headline features:

- **AI-Native Infinite Canvas:** A redesigned workspace where text, images, code snippets, and reference screenshots can all coexist on a single surface, replacing the single-screen generation of the original tool.
- **Smarter Design Agent:** An agent that reasons across the entire project's evolution — handling layout decisions, component selection, and iterative refinement based on full project context rather than isolated prompts.
- **Voice Interaction:** Users can speak directly to the canvas. The agent listens, offers real-time design critiques, asks clarifying questions, and executes changes live — such as generating three alternative menu layouts or switching a screen to a dark palette, all by voice.
- **Instant Prototyping:** Stitch can now generate up to five screens simultaneously and link them into interactive, clickable prototypes with a single "Play" button. It also auto-generates logical next screens based on user flows.
- **DESIGN.md and Design Systems:** A new agent-friendly markdown format that documents design rules, allowing teams to export and re-import a design system across projects or into third-party tools.

## The Developer Pipeline

Google has built a dedicated SDK and MCP (Model Context Protocol) server for Stitch, connecting it directly to coding assistants including Claude Code, Gemini CLI, and Cursor. Designs can flow from Stitch into these environments without context-switching, enabling a continuous pipeline from visual concept to production code. Stitch exports to Figma as editable layers with Auto Layout, and also outputs clean HTML/CSS and Tailwind-compatible code. Integration with Google AI Studio and the Antigravity development environment is also supported.

## Market Impact: Figma Shares Drop

The market read the update as a direct competitive move against Figma. Shares of Figma Inc. fell as much as 8% on March 19 following the announcement, according to reporting by Winbuzzer and SiliconANGLE. Figma's platform remains the industry standard for collaborative UI design, but Stitch's ability to export Figma-compatible files — combined with its free pricing and Google's distribution scale — positions it as a credible, low-friction alternative, particularly for early-stage product work, founders prototyping MVPs, and developers who have historically avoided dedicated design tools.

## Pricing and Access

Stitch remains free during its beta period. Current usage limits are 350 standard generations and 200 pro generations per month. No paid tier has been announced, though analysts project pricing around $25–$30/month if Google introduces a commercial plan — in line with competitors such as Galileo AI and UX Pilot. The tool is available now at [stitch.withgoogle.com](https://stitch.withgoogle.com).

## The Takeaway

Google's transformation of Stitch from a lightweight prototyping experiment into a full "vibe design" platform is the most significant move in the AI design tools space this month. It lowers the barrier for non-designers to produce high-fidelity, interactive UI prototypes and closes meaningful ground on Figma's workflow dominance. For teams building mobile apps today, Stitch is now a legitimate starting point — especially for exploration and early-stage iteration — before handing off to production tools.

For AI-native design platforms like [floow.design](https://floow.design), this signals exactly what the market is demanding: less friction between idea and interface, more intelligence baked into the canvas itself.
